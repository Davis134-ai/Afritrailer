Our History

Inspired by the Association for Visual Arts (AVA)

AfriTrailer draws inspiration from the rich legacy of African arts organizations that have championed creative expression across the continent for generations.

1850

The South African Fine Arts Society was founded in Cape Town — the first organized art body in the country. The original society sought to establish a permanent art gallery and library to exhibit and promote art for the public good.

1945

The organization evolved, becoming the South African Association of Arts (SAAA) and serving as a national body amalgamating art societies across South Africa.

1971

The Western Cape headquarters moved into its current location at 35 Church Street, Cape Town, a space that has remained a home for African arts ever since.

1995

The Western Cape branch reconstituted itself as the Association for Visual Arts (AVA) — an independent, autonomous non-profit organization with a renewed focus on cultural interaction and support for contemporary art.

2001

AVA secured a long-term partnership with Spier, whose support has been vital in sustaining the organization's role in arts development.

2004-2006

The AVA received official NPO status in 2004 and Public Benefit Organisation status in 2006.

Today

Just as AVA remains the oldest non-profit art gallery in Cape Town, AfriTrailer aims to become the premier digital platform championing contemporary African visual storytelling — from film and documentaries to digital content across all media.

Our Vision & Mission

At its core, AfriTrailer is dedicated to advancing and promoting contemporary African visual storytelling and empowering content creators across the continent. As a platform committed to African cinema and digital media:

Advances Creators

We support creators from all backgrounds and heritages, empowering creative expression in its widest possible form.

Showcases African Stories

We house contemporary African visual storytelling, embracing innovation across traditional and new media.

Creates Inclusive Space

We occupy a neutral, inclusive space within Africa's creative ecosystem, welcoming diverse artistic voices and practices.

Builds Community

We engage with the wider creative community, including filmmakers, producers, investors, and cultural institutions.

Increases Awareness

We increase public awareness of African cinema through trailers, behind-the-scenes content, and creator spotlights.

Empowers Economically

We support creators through revenue opportunities, exposure, and development programs that make filmmaking sustainable.
